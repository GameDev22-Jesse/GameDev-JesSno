History and Impact of Photography: 2000 to 2022







Photography Improvements

2000

The S1-Pro was released as the first consumer-level DSLR with a 3 megapixel sensor and a Nikon film body. It was listed as $3,500 instead of $10,000+, and a few months later, the DSLR became more defining than the film cameras, which only had 2 megapixel CCD sensors and went through AA batteries rapidly.


2004

The Epson R-D1 was the first to become a mirrorless camera and have am electronic viewfinder at the cost of having large sensors and a poor battery life.


2008

The EOS 5D MARK II was a high-definition video camera for less than $3,000, which revolutionized the film industry by allowing high quality video cameras available to more.
This was the same year that Apple produced their first iPhone, which had a rear facing camera of 2 megapixels and a video quality of 640x480 pixels that recorded at 30 frames per second. It is not much compared to now, but it was a massive step in the right direction.


2010

Although the film industry was clearly moving into the digital era, the most affordable for most people was still film. Fuji Velvia 50 specifically was one of the most used film around this time.


2022

Apple has been producing multiple versions of their iPhone ever since 2008, each being better or different than the last, and the iPhone available to consumers in 2022 is the iPhone 13 Pro.
It contains:

  • Aperture options of f/1.5, f/1.8, and f/2.8.
  • A zoom up to 15x.
  • A Night Mode option for shooting photos or videos at night or in dark spaces.

Changes in Methods

Methods then:

  • Portraits

    • Portraits were very popular with the iPhone out, which were equipped with portable cameras.

  • Realism

    • Realism photography captured life and showed it to viewers in a way that they never saw before and showed people different cultures and ways of looking at things.

  • Historical Events

    • Historical events are always popular, but increased in popularity when World War 2 occured.



Methods now:

  • Masked Up

    • With the pandemic occuring and spreading rapidly, photos are crucial to show people how it is effecting our society.

  • Aerial

    • New technology that is available to everyone is being used to capture photos of the land from the sky without the use of planes or aircrafts.

  • Astrophotography

    • Newer cameras are allowing people to take photos of space, allowing astronomers and astronauts to further discover and research it without actually going to space and risking lives.

  • Event

    • People are still taking pictures of occurences since it is vital to record our history and spread information as wide as possible.

  • Macro

    • With high definition cameras being available and able to manufacture, people are taking pictures of microscopic organisms to study how they work.

  • Sports

    • With a high shutter speed, cameras allow us to capture moments you would only see in a split second and allow us to further examine the moment to its finest detail.

  • Travel

    • Travel photography allows people to see whole different parts of the world without actually going there, and that also brings them information for them to use when deciding where they could go and how they would get there.

  • Wildlife

    • Since there is no 100% safe way to watch and see how animals in the wild interact with each other, cameras with far zooms and professionals who are able to track animals are able to take photos and allow us to see them, when otherwise we wouldn't be able to.

  • Still Life

    • With a wider array of options on cameras allowing you to affect how long the shutter is open before it closes lets us take photos of things that would usually be moving and seemingly be stopped in time, with little to no motion blur.

The How, Where and Why of Photographs and their Purpose

How

A DSLR camera works by letting light into the camera through the lens, reflected off a mirror inside the cameras body, and through another mirror that leads the light to the viewfinder, where you can see the what photo the camera will be taking. The picture isn’t taken until the shutter button is pressed and moves the mirror out of the way to the imaging sensor.

Where

A photo can be taken anywhere there is light, and there is even light in space, so you are literally only limited by your imagination.

Taking a picture:

  • Outdoors:

    • The purpose of outdoor photography is to capture the baeuty of the great outdoors. Your photo captures the mood, scenery, and setting of your focused subject.

  • Indoors:

    • The purpose of indoor photography is to continue to shoot photos inside th comfort of your own home on a rainy day or to protect your equipment from harsh weather. Indoor photography also works great when wanting to work with ambient space.

  • In Space:

    • Astrophotography means to take pictures of space and everything in/around it. The purpose of taking such photos is to help astronomers learn more about a planet or star's atmosphere, life support capability, and predict changes in space and weather. Space photography is required when understanding the composition and fate of our universe.


Why

They are confirmations of our personal experiences and showcase said experiences from our point of view. Photos are taken as a way to tell a story, as a way to reflect on who we and others are, but people take pictures for all types of reasons, ranging from keeping a certain feeling to remembering a life event.
